Learning as I go...Reexplore my page every few weeks to see new projects I'm working on, made with lines of code I finally wrapped my head around, and displayed with some of my own imagination. In other words, next time you land here, chances are this page will have taken new artistic direction from its curator (me). If we trace through history we'll find a literal example of our innate function, or desire, as humans to communicate. That's always remained constant, but the mediums we use to deliver our messages, or artistic expressions, have evolved throughout history; from the cave paintings of the Lascaux Cave in France, to Cuneiform being etched into clay tablets in Mesopotamia, to Gutenberg's Printing Press, to Morse's Telegraph, to Marconi's Radio, to Farnsworth's Television. The Internet as a medium is extraordinary in many regards, but what fascinates me is the ability to change a message. A history textbook can't rewrite itself with current events, an entirely new book must be printed with the updated information. It's the ability to edit, build upon, or even reinvent the original message entirely that excites me; there's a fluidity to message conveying that eluded its technological predecessors. I hope you'll discover a parallel as you browse the site. As I said, I'm learning as I go and that's the message I'm trying to convey. At any given point in time this site should reflect what I'm learning
